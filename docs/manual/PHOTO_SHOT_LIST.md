# Field Guide — Photo Checklist

The complete shooting list: one repeatable setup, then every shot to capture. Check items off as
you go. Hero plates are the priority (one per form chapter). The cover and atlas macros are listed
after.

When you export, name each file as shown in `code` and upload to `Field_Guide` →
`docs/manual/diagrams/`. I will wire the hero photos into the chapters the same way we did the
diagrams (drop the PNG, I rebuild).

---

## The setup (shoot all heroes the same way)

Consistency is what makes 30 photos look like a designed system instead of snapshots. Lock this
once and keep it identical for every hero:

- **Angle:** three-quarter view, camera at piece mid-height. Same angle every time.
- **Framing:** whole form in frame, generous margin, shoot a little wide so we can crop to a uniform 6×9.
- **Light:** soft and directional, raking slightly across the surface (north-facing window, or one large softbox at about 45 degrees). The rake reveals grain, wear, and construction. Avoid hard direct sun.
- **Background:** plain warm backdrop in our palette (canvas, muslin, or a simple plastered wall) in parchment or kraft tones. No busy wallpaper.
- **Accessories:** one or two period-appropriate pieces at most, never crossing the form's outline or hiding the legs, feet, drawer fronts, or joinery.
- **Camera:** shoot raw, fixed white balance, lowest ISO, tripod. So all 30 share one tone.

## Cover

- [ ] **Cover hero** `cover-hero.png` — your one showpiece, shot in the full rich period setting with great light. An instantly-readable icon works best (Golden Oak dresser with mirror, a roll-top, or a strong Windsor). This is the one place to go all-in on environment and mood. Shoot vertical, leave headroom at the top and space at the bottom for the title and subtitle.

## Workflow reminder

1. Shoot a section in one sitting (same setup, same light) so the set matches.
2. Export each as the `code` name above, watching the Windows `.png.png` trap.
3. Upload a batch to `docs/manual/diagrams/` on `Field_Guide`, tell me, I rebuild.
4. Heroes flow into the chapters; the cover and macros wire the same way.

**Count:** 30 hero plates (3 multi-shot, ~34 frames) + 1 cover + up to 7 optional atlas macros.
