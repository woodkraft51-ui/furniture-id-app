# Field Guide — Photo Shot List (hero plates)

Every form chapter opens with a hero plate: one clear photo of your own piece. This is the full
list of owner photos the book needs, by section. Shoot three-quarter, well-lit, generous margin,
on a plain background unless a note says otherwise. Pieces marked **(multi-shot)** need more than
one frame.

Diagrams and decision-tree art are tracked separately in `ILLUSTRATION_LIST.md`; this file is
only the hero photographs.

## Part II — The Forms

### Seating (6)
- Windsor chair — three-quarter, profile so splayed legs, spindle back, plank seat read
- Rocking chair — profile, so the rockers read
- Morris chair — three-quarter, the back-support rod visible
- Wing chair — three-quarter
- Settee — three-quarter, full width
- Victorian sofa — three-quarter, full length

### Tables (7)
- Parlor table — three-quarter
- Drop-leaf table — **(multi-shot)** one leaf up and one down
- Gateleg table — open, a gate swung out
- Tilt-top table — **(multi-shot)** one flat, one tilted vertical
- Pembroke table — leaves up, drawer slightly open
- Candle stand — three-quarter
- Work table — three-quarter, the worn top reading

### Case and Storage (10)
- Chest of drawers — three-quarter, full height
- Dresser — three-quarter, with mirror
- Dressing table / vanity — three-quarter, with mirror
- Washstand — three-quarter, splash back and towel bar visible
- Sideboard — three-quarter, full width
- China cabinet — three-quarter, glass doors visible
- Corner cabinet — three-quarter so the angled sides read
- Hoosier cabinet — three-quarter, doors and work surface visible
- Blanket chest — three-quarter, lid and base feet visible
- Armoire / wardrobe — full height, a door open

### Desks (3)
- Slant-front desk — open, the fitted interior showing
- Secretary desk — full height, fall open
- Roll-top / cylinder desk — cover open, fitted interior visible

### Beds (2)
- Iron or brass bed — head and foot
- Wooden bedstead — head and foot, side rail visible

### Everyday Pieces (2)
- Telephone stand — three-quarter
- Nightstand — three-quarter, ideally a matching pair

### Clocks (1, multi-piece)
- Clocks — **(multi-shot)** a tall-case clock and a shelf clock, side by side

## Part III — Reference Atlases (optional macro shots)

These are not hero plates but raise the atlases from good to professional. Lower priority than the
heroes; Claude line-art can stand in where you do not have a photo.

- Saw and tool marks — pit-saw vs circular vs band-saw marks (three macro crops); a circular-saw
  arc on a secondary surface; drawknife facets vs a sanded-smooth reproduction
- Fasteners, joinery, finishes, hardware — macro crops are welcome wherever you have a clean example

## Count

30 hero photos, one per form chapter. Three of them are multi-shot (drop-leaf, tilt-top, clocks),
so budget roughly 33 to 34 frames for the heroes. Atlas macros are a bonus pass on top.
