## Rule it out in 10

A wooden bedstead is a **wood-framed sleeping support**: a wooden headboard, usually a footboard, and side rails, with a slat, rope, or platform mattress support.

If the head and foot are metal castings or tubing, that is an iron bed. **Metal side rails do not change the answer**: a wood head and foot make it a bedstead, even with iron rails (this is the companion to the iron-bed lesson).

## What it is

The bedstead is the foundational wooden bed, made in every American period. A **complete bedstead is more than a loose headboard**, it is the full frame. Within the form live the familiar subtypes, and recognizing them is most of the work:

- **Four-poster**, four tall corner posts, sometimes for a canopy.
- **Tester and half-tester**, with a full or partial overhead canopy frame.
- **Sleigh bed**, scrolled head and foot curving outward, Empire and later.
- **Spool and cannonball**, turned posts of stacked spools or topped with a ball, common mid-1800s.
- **Panel bed**, flat paneled head and foot, the everyday late Victorian and early-1900s suite bed.

## On the short list, confirm and date it

- Identify the **subtype** from the list, which carries period character (spool and sleigh point mid-1800s, panel beds point late Victorian to early modern suites).
- Date by **construction**: joinery on the head and foot, the **rail-to-post joint** (mortise, bolt, or hook hardware), wood, saw marks, and fasteners (Method section).
- Beds are often **shortened, widened, or rebuilt** to fit modern mattresses. Check the rails and joints for alteration.

## Do not confuse it with

| Form | What it really is | The tell that separates it |
|---|---|---|
| **Wooden bedstead** | Wood-frame bed | Wood head and foot, even with metal rails |
| Iron bed | Metal-frame bed | Metal castings or tubing head and foot |
| Daybed | Reclining or sleeping seat | Single long side, seat-and-sleep use |
| Loose headboard | Decorative panel only | No complete frame, not a full bedstead |

## What hurts the value
- **Conversion**: rails cut, lengthened, or replaced for a modern mattress.
- **A married head and foot**, or a head sold alone as a "bed."
- **Refinishing** on a fine early or original-surface example.

## What it is worth, in plain terms
A complete early **four-poster, sleigh, or fine spool bed** with original rails is **desirable**. A late Victorian or early-1900s **panel bed** is **common and modest**, especially as part of a suite. Completeness, original rails, and an honest surface set the range. (Note the recurring market reality: beds without original rails are hard to sell.)

## Quick field routine
First, confirm wood head and foot, metal rails do not matter.
Second, name the subtype.
Third, date by construction and the rail joint.
Fourth, check for conversion and marriage.
