# The Telephone Stand
*also called: telephone table, telephone cabinet (enclosed variant); see also gossip bench*

## Rule it out in 10

A telephone stand is a **compact stand or small cabinet made to hold a telephone**, with a small top scaled to a phone (about 8 to 14 inches) and a **shelf, drawer, or compartment for the directory**.

Here is the affirmation test, and it matters: **a small stand is not a telephone stand just because it is small.** Without phone-era evidence (a phone-scaled top, directory storage), it is just a side table. Require the telephone purpose before you name it.

## What it is

The telephone stand is purpose-built furniture from the wired-telephone decades, roughly 1900 onward. It is compact and tall enough to use standing or from a seat, with a small top for the phone and a lower **shelf or drawer for the directory**. The enclosed version is a **telephone cabinet**, where the phone sits in or atop a small closed compartment.

One important cousin: the **gossip bench**, the little seat-plus-phone-shelf combination, is actually a **telephone desk**, not a telephone stand, because it adds integrated seating and a writing surface. (Our own software once called a child's high chair a "telephone bench" on a seat-plus-surface alone, which is exactly the mistake this affirmation test prevents.)

## On the short list, confirm and date it

- Confirm **telephone-era evidence**: a phone-scaled top and directory storage, not just small size.
- Date by **construction**: oak and Golden Oak vocabulary point to the earlier decades, waterfall and Art Deco lines point to the 1930s, and machine construction throughout is expected (Method section).

## Do not confuse it with

| Form | What it really is | The tell that separates it |
|---|---|---|
| **Telephone stand** | Compact phone-and-directory stand | Phone-scaled top plus directory storage |
| Gossip bench (telephone desk) | Seat plus phone shelf and writing surface | Integrated seat, it is a desk form |
| Side table | General utility table beside seating | No telephone purpose or directory storage |
| Nightstand | Bedside case | Bedside placement and scale |

## What hurts the value
- **Calling a plain side table a telephone stand** with no phone evidence (and the reverse).
- **Replaced hardware** and refinishing.

## What it is worth, in plain terms
Most telephone stands are **common and modest**, charming and very saleable for their nostalgia. A nicely styled oak or Art Deco example, or a complete gossip bench, sits a step higher. Style, condition, and completeness set the range.

## Quick field routine
First, confirm real telephone purpose: phone-scaled top, directory storage.
Second, if it has a seat, it is a gossip bench (telephone desk), not a stand.
Third, date by style and construction.
Fourth, do not name it on small size alone.
