## Rule it out in 10

A candle stand is a **small, tall, light stand with a tiny top**, commonly 8 to 18 inches across, built to hold **one object**: a candle, a lamp, or a plant. It is highly movable and has essentially no storage.

If the top is large enough for tea service or display of several things, it is a tilt-top or tea table. If it is a full table with storage, it is something else. The candle stand is defined by **small scale and single-object support**.

## What it is

The candle stand is the smallest of the pedestal tables, made to carry light from room to room before fixed lighting. Most are **pedestal or tripod** stands with a slender turned shaft and three feet, and many of the best **tilt** like their larger tilt-top cousins. The form runs the whole American range, which means, as with the tilt-top, that **eighteenth-century originals and Colonial Revival copies look alike** and must be told apart by construction.

## On the short list, confirm and date it

- Confirm **small scale** and single-object support.
- If it tilts, **flip it and read the underside**, the same evidence as the tilt-top: latch, birdcage, wear arc, oxidation, fasteners.
- Date by **construction**: shaft turning, foot carving, tool marks, and wear (Method section). Machine-uniform turning and modern fasteners point to revival. When unsure, the period-or-reproduction tree applies.

## Do not confuse it with

| Form | What it really is | The tell that separates it |
|---|---|---|
| **Candle stand** | Small single-object support stand | Tiny top, tall, light, movable |
| Tilt-top table | Larger tilting pedestal table | Bigger top for service or display |
| Tea table | Service-oriented table | Larger, made for tea service |
| Plant stand | Object stand (overlaps) | Often taller and narrower, later use |

## What hurts the value
- **A replaced top or married base**, common on small light stands.
- **A revival piece presented as eighteenth-century**.

## What it is worth, in plain terms
A documented **eighteenth-century candle stand**, especially a fine tripod with good carving, is **premium**. A **Colonial Revival** candle stand is **common and modest**. Underside evidence and construction, not silhouette, set the range.

## Quick field routine
First, confirm small scale and single-object support.
Second, if it tilts, read the underside.
Third, date by construction and turning, not the antique look.
Fourth, decide period or revival with the tree.
