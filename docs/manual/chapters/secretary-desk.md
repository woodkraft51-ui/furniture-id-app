# The Secretary Desk
*also called: secretary, secretaire, bureau bookcase, secretary bookcase*

`[ERA BAND: 1840 ██████████ then mostly Colonial Revival. The form runs c. 1700 to present, with a strong revival after 1880.]`

`[HERO PLATE: your own secretary, full height, fall open. Wide canvas, generous margin.]`

## Rule it out in 10

A secretary desk is a **two-part piece: a desk section with a fitted writing interior below, and a bookcase or cabinet on top**. Tall, vertical, two stacked cases.

If it is a single desk with no upper bookcase, it is a slant-front or fall-front desk. If the cover rolls, it is a roll-top. The secretary is the **desk-plus-bookcase tower**.

## What it is

The secretary combines writing and storage in one tall piece: a lower desk, often with a **slant fall front or a drop front**, opening to a fitted interior of pigeonholes and small drawers, set over a chest of drawers, with an **upper bookcase** above, frequently **glazed** with wood mullions or fitted with solid paneled doors. It is the formal writing-and-display piece of the home, made from about 1700 to the present and revived strongly in the Colonial Revival after 1880.

## On the short list, confirm and date it

- Confirm the **two-part desk-over-bookcase architecture** and the **fitted writing interior**.
- Check the two halves **belong together**: matching wood, finish, proportion, and wear where the bookcase sits on the desk. Secretaries are a classic **marriage** risk, an old desk topped with a later or unrelated bookcase.
- Date by **construction** as always, hand versus machine joinery, fasteners, glazing bar construction, and secondary woods (Method section). Period and revival look alike, so build evidence, not silhouette.

## Do not confuse it with

| Form | What it really is | The tell that separates it |
|---|---|---|
| **Secretary desk** | Desk below, bookcase above | Two-part, fitted desk interior plus a bookcase top |
| Slant-front desk | Single desk, sloping lid | No bookcase top |
| Bookcase | Shelving only | No writing interior |
| Roll-top desk | Desk with a rolling cover | Moving cover, single case |

## What hurts the value
- **A married top and bottom**, the most common and most important issue here.
- **Replaced glazing or interior**, and refinishing on a fine period example.

## What it is worth, in plain terms
A genuine **period secretary** with matching original halves and a good interior is **premium, sometimes strongly so**. A **Colonial Revival** secretary is **moderate**, and a **married** example is worth less than either honest piece would be alone. Matching halves, original surface, and construction set the range.

## Quick field routine
First, confirm desk-below, bookcase-above, with a fitted interior.
Second, prove the two halves are an honest pair, not a marriage.
Third, date by construction.
Fourth, check the interior and glazing are original.
