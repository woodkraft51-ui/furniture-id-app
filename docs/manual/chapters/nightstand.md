## Rule it out in 10

A nightstand is a **small bedside case at mattress or bed-rail height**, with a top for a lamp, book, clock, or glass of water, and usually a small drawer, shelf, or little cabinet below.

If it is a low table beside seating, that is an end table. If it is a larger storage case, it is a chest or small cabinet. The nightstand is defined by **bedside scale and immediate-access use**.

## What it is

The nightstand is bedside furniture, sized for reach from the bed. Surface comes first (lamp and clock and book), with storage secondary. From the late nineteenth century and especially in twentieth-century coordinated suites, it was made **in pairs** to flank the bed and match the dresser and chest. That suite context is a useful identification and dating clue: a nightstand whose wood, hardware, and style match a known suite places it with that suite.

## On the short list, confirm and date it

- Confirm **bedside scale** and surface-first use.
- Look for a **matching pair or suite**, which both confirms the form and helps date it.
- Date by **construction** as always: joinery, fasteners, secondary woods, hardware (Method section). Style follows the bedroom-suite fashion of its decade.

## Do not confuse it with

| Form | What it really is | The tell that separates it |
|---|---|---|
| **Nightstand** | Small bedside case | Bedside scale, surface-first, often paired |
| End table | Small seating-area table | Living-room placement and use |
| Side table | General small table | Not specifically bedside |
| Small chest or cabinet | Storage-first small case | Storage dominates over surface and access |

## What hurts the value
- **A split pair** (a single sells for less than the matched two).
- **Replaced hardware** and refinishing, and a nightstand **separated from its suite**.

## What it is worth, in plain terms
Single nightstands are **common and modest**. A **matched pair**, especially with its dresser and bed as a suite, is worth meaningfully more together. Pairing, suite completeness, and original hardware set the range.

## Quick field routine
First, confirm bedside scale and surface-first use.
Second, look for a matching pair or suite.
Third, date by construction and suite style.
Fourth, keep pairs together, they are worth more as two.
