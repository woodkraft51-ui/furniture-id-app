# The Drop-Leaf Table
*also called: leaf table, falling-leaf table; family includes gateleg and Pembroke*

## Rule it out in 10

A drop-leaf table has **hinged leaves attached to a fixed center section that hang down when closed and lift to lock horizontal** when open. The hinge-and-leaf is the defining mechanism.

If the table expands by pulling apart and dropping in separate boards, that is an extension table, not a drop-leaf. The drop-leaf grows by raising leaves that were always attached.

## What it is

The drop-leaf is the space-saver of the household: narrow against a wall when closed, a full surface when the leaves come up. The leaves meet the center on a **rule joint** (a shaped, interlocking hinge edge) and are held up by a support that varies by type:

- **Swing legs** that pivot out (this is the gateleg subtype, its own chapter).
- **Brackets or fly rails** that fold out.
- Simple **swing supports**.

It can serve as a dining, kitchen, tea, or occasional table depending on size. Two refined members of the family get their own chapters: the **gateleg** (swing-leg gates) and the **Pembroke** (compact, with a drawer).

## On the short list, confirm and date it

- Confirm the **rule joint** and the **leaf supports**.
- Date by **construction**, since the form spans centuries: hinge type, secondary woods, saw marks, fasteners, and the **wear arcs** on the underside where supports have swung for decades. (Method section.)

## Do not confuse it with

| Form | What it really is | The tell that separates it |
|---|---|---|
| **Drop-leaf table** | Hinged leaves that lift and lock | Attached leaves, rule joint, fold-out support |
| Extension table | Expands with slides and inserts | Pulls apart, adds separate boards |
| Gateleg table | Drop-leaf with swing-leg gates | Swinging leg-and-stretcher assemblies |
| Pembroke table | Compact drop-leaf with a drawer | Small, refined, drawer in the apron |

## What hurts the value
- **Replaced leaves or re-hinged** joints, and warped or cracked leaves.
- **Married or cut-down** tops and bases.

## What it is worth, in plain terms
Plain drop-leaf tables are **common and modest**, everyday and useful. Value rises with **age, good wood, and honest construction**, and with the more refined family members (a fine early gateleg or Federal Pembroke). Construction and originality, as always.

## Quick field routine
First, confirm the lift-and-lock leaves and the rule joint.
Second, identify the support type, which may name it a gateleg.
Third, date by construction and underside wear.
Fourth, check the leaves and supports are original.
