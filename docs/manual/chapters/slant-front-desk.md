# The Slant-Front Desk
*also called: slant-top desk, fall-front desk, bureau desk, desk-on-frame*

`[ERA BAND: 1840 ██████████ then mostly Colonial Revival. The form is the standard desk c. 1700 to 1860, heavily revived after 1880.]`

`[HERO PLATE: your own slant-front, open, the fitted interior showing. Wide canvas, generous margin.]`

## Rule it out in 10

A slant-front desk has a **sloping lid that hinges down to form the writing surface**, opening to reveal a **fitted interior** of pigeonholes and small drawers, usually over a chest of drawers below.

If the cover rolls or curves, it is a roll-top or cylinder desk. If there is a bookcase on top, it is a secretary. The slant-front is defined by the **sloping fall-down lid**.

## What it is

The slant-front was the standard American writing desk of the colonial and Federal years, roughly 1700 to 1860. The sloping lid drops on hinges, often supported by pull-out **lopers**, to make a writing surface, and behind it sits a fitted interior of **pigeonholes, document slots, and little drawers**, sometimes with a central prospect door. Below is a case of graduated drawers. It was revived heavily in the Colonial Revival from about 1880, so most examples you meet are revival rather than period.

## On the short list, confirm and date it

- Confirm the **sloping fall lid, the lopers, and the fitted interior**.
- Date by **construction**, since period and revival look alike: hand-cut versus machine dovetails on the drawers and interior, cut versus wire nails, saw marks, secondary woods, and the wear on the lopers and lid (Method section).
- Hand work and early fasteners point to the colonial or Federal period; uniform machine work points to Colonial Revival. Run the period-or-reproduction tree when unsure.

## Do not confuse it with

| Form | What it really is | The tell that separates it |
|---|---|---|
| **Slant-front desk** | Desk with a sloping fall lid | Sloping hinged lid over a chest |
| Secretary desk | Slant or fall front plus a bookcase top | A bookcase or cabinet above |
| Roll-top or cylinder desk | Desk with a rolling or curved cover | Moving cover, not a hinged slope |
| Drop-front (butler's) desk | Vertical fall front | Flat front drops flat, not sloped |

## What hurts the value
- **A replaced lid, lopers, or interior**, and a **married** top and base (desk-on-frame marriages are common).
- **Refinishing** on a fine period example.

## What it is worth, in plain terms
A genuine **colonial or Federal slant-front**, especially in figured mahogany or walnut with a good interior, is **premium**. A **Colonial Revival** slant-front, which is what you will usually find, is **moderate to modest**. Construction dating and originality, not the colonial look, set the range.

## Quick field routine
First, confirm the sloping fall lid, lopers, and fitted interior.
Second, date by drawer and interior construction.
Third, decide period or revival with the tree.
Fourth, check for a desk-on-frame marriage and replaced parts.
