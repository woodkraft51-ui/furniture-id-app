# Phase 5 — Go-to-Market & Pricing Plan

> Ready-to-execute at launch (target Jun 17). Principle: near-zero production cost +
> ~90% margin → protect that margin (free channels first, small paid tests only) and
> build the asset (email list) from day one. The Father's Day spike is a bonus; the
> evergreen long tail + funnel are the real ROI.

## Product & positioning
- **Title:** *Field Guide to American Furniture Identification, 1840–1940 — Spotting, Dating & Valuing the Antiques You'll Actually Find.*
- **Promise:** learn to identify, date, and value real antiques in the field — the skill, not just pictures.
- **Complete-in-itself** (not a teaser). Encyclopedia Edition = future founder's-upgrade (see Email).

## Pricing (digital PDF)
- **Launch / Father's Day price: $17** (impulse/gift sweet spot for a ~90-page illustrated guide).
- **Founder's framing:** "$17 launch price — rising after Father's Day" (urgency, honest).
- **Tier later (post-launch, lifts average order value, zero extra production):**
  - *Basic* $12 — guide only.
  - *Field Kit* $24 — guide + printable quick-reference cheat sheets (wood/fastener/joinery one-pagers pulled from the atlases).
- **Bundle hook:** "Buy now → free upgrade to the Complete Encyclopedia Edition when it launches."

## Platforms (pick 1–2; all low-fee, instant digital delivery)
- **Gumroad** — simplest, ~10% fee, built for digital, easy email capture. *Recommended primary.*
- **Etsy** — strong organic discovery for vintage/antique buyers; $0.20 listing + ~6.5% + payment fee. *Recommended secondary for SEO traffic.*
- **Own landing page** (later) — best margin + list ownership.

## Channels — FREE FIRST (protect the margin)
1. **Pinterest** *(highest-leverage, free, perfect fit).* A visual furniture guide is native to Pinterest. Pin each form's silhouette plate + "how to date it" tip → link to the listing. Start now; it compounds.
2. **Facebook groups** — antique, estate-sale, thrifting, "what is this furniture?" groups. Be helpful (answer ID questions), soft-link the guide. No spam.
3. **Owner's dealer network** — warm, immediate, and credibility-building before the app.
4. **Reddit** (r/Antiques, r/whatisthisthing, r/vintage) — value-first participation.
5. **Marketplace cross-promo** — every furniture flip listing mentions the guide.

## Paid — SMALL TEST ONLY, after free channels prove the copy
- **Cap an initial test at $50–$100.** Pinterest or FB, tightly targeted (antique collectors, estate-sale interest).
- **Rule:** scale spend only if a channel returns > its cost on a $17 product. A $15-net product cannot absorb expensive cold clicks — do NOT "blast" blind.

## Email capture (the real asset — set up day one)
- Offer a **free lead magnet** (e.g., the 1-page "Fastener & Nail Dating Cheat Sheet") in exchange for email.
- Every buyer auto-added. Tag buyers vs. leads.
- This list monetizes the **Encyclopedia Edition, the teaching app, and the ID app** later. Worth more than launch revenue.

## Launch sequence
- **By Jun 16:** listing live (Gumroad), sales copy + the subtitle as hook, 3–5 sample interior pages as preview, lead-magnet signup wired.
- **Jun 17:** announce to network + start pinning + post in 2–3 groups. Begin tiny paid test.
- **Jun 17–21:** Father's Day push ("done shopping? gift the antique-lover in your life — instant download").
- **Post-FD:** raise to standard price; let Pinterest/Etsy SEO carry the evergreen tail.

## What "good" looks like (honest KPIs)
- FD window: realistic 100–250 sales (~$1.5–3.75k) *if* marketed; modest 30–75 if passive. Don't bank on a windfall.
- Real win: **15–40 sales/month evergreen** (~$225–600/mo passive) + a growing email list.
