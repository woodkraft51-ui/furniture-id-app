# Launch Playbook — A Field Guide to American Furniture

> Strategy: you do not have a budget problem, you have a placement problem, and placement is free.
> Publish where buyers already search (Amazon), ignite with your own network, and let Pinterest
> compound. Hold almost all of your cash. Sell the direct PDF later for margin and an email list.
> Book is a finished ~207-page full-color illustrated reference. Scope is identify and date (value
> is guidance, not a promise).

---

## The five moves, in order

1. **Publish on Amazon KDP (cost: $0).** Color paperback + Kindle ebook. Amazon is your traffic.
2. **Win the listing (cost: $0, decisive).** Keyword subtitle, 7 keywords, categories, Look Inside on.
3. **Ignite with your network (cost: $0, most important).** Father's Day. First 10 to 25 sales and
   honest reviews in week one. That early signal is what makes Amazon start ranking you.
4. **Pinterest is the engine (cost: $0, compounding).** Your diagrams and spreads are perfect pins.
5. **Hold cash for one small Amazon Ads test ($5 to $10/day),** started only after the listing converts.

Do NOT spend on Facebook or Google ads. Cold audience + niche + tiny budget = wasted.

### Book description (Amazon; bold the lead lines)
**Is it a genuine period antique, or a clever reproduction? And what makes one worth ten times the other?**

Most furniture guides just show you pictures. This one teaches you to *read* a piece the way a seasoned appraiser does, in the field, in seconds.

Covering the American antiques you will actually meet from **1840 to 1940** (Victorian, Eastlake, Golden Oak, Mission, and Art Deco), this fully illustrated reference walks you through a simple, repeatable method: rule it out fast, read the construction, and stack the evidence.

**Inside you will find:**
- 30+ form chapters, each with a 10-second "rule it out" test and a careful "confirm it" path
- Reference atlases on fasteners, joinery, saw and tool marks, wood species, hardware, and finishes, the tells that actually date a piece
- Decision trees, period style timeline, and side-by-side comparisons that separate look-alikes
- Clear guidance on what drives value and what destroys it (original surface, marriages, replaced parts)

Whether you are an **antique dealer, estate liquidator, appraiser, collector, or picker**, this is the field guide that turns "I think it's old" into "I know what it is, when it was made, and why it matters."

*Read the dovetails. Read the nails. Read the wear. Then call it with confidence.*

## Pinterest captions (one per pin; pin the matching diagram/spread)

1. **Period or reproduction? Run this tree.** Modern screws and machine joinery give a fake away fast. Save this antique-dating checklist. #antiques #antiquefurniture
2. **Three nails, three eras.** Hand-wrought, cut, or wire, each one pins a date on the board it's in. The fastest age clue in furniture. #antiquedealer #estatesale
3. **Date a chair by its legs.** Turned, cabriole, ball-and-claw, tapered: each points to a period. A visual cheat sheet. #antiquefurniture #vintagedecor
4. **Hand-cut vs machine-cut dovetails.** People are slightly irregular; machines are perfect. Pull a drawer and look. #antiques #furniturerestoration
5. **A curved saw mark means it's not before the 1830s.** No matter how "colonial" it looks. Read the hidden surfaces. #antiquedealer #pickers
6. **Know your wood.** Walnut, oak, mahogany, cherry, and the secondary woods hiding inside the drawer. #antiquefurniture #woodworking
7. **Casters by period.** Porcelain, cast iron, brass cup, rubber: the wheel under the leg can date a piece (or expose a swap). #antiques #estatesale
8. **American furniture styles, 1840 to 1940, in one timeline.** From Rococo Revival to Art Deco. Pin it for the booth. #antiquedealer #vintage
9. **The original surface is the prize.** Stripping an old finish can destroy most of the value. Here's how to read crazing and patina. #antiques #furniturerestoration
10. **Is it a Hoosier or a pantry cupboard?** One decision tree settles it. #antiquefurniture #farmhouse

Each pin links to the Amazon listing (week 1, to seed rank) or the direct PDF (ongoing, for margin + email).

## Launch checklist

**This week (after micro-edits):**
- [ ] Finalize cover to KDP's exact trim + bleed + spine spec (spine width depends on final page count and paper). KDP gives you a template once you know the page count.
- [ ] Compress the interior images for the Kindle file; keep it small.
- [ ] Create the KDP paperback: upload interior PDF + cover, set price, categories, keywords, subtitle.
- [ ] Create the Kindle ebook: same metadata; do NOT enroll in Select if selling PDF direct.
- [ ] Turn on Look Inside; confirm Part I and a sample chapter are in the preview.
- [ ] Order proof copies.

**Launch week (Father's Day):**
- [ ] Announce to your dealer network, point them to the Amazon listing.
- [ ] Ask early buyers for honest reviews (you need the first handful badly).
- [ ] Start pinning, two or three pins, then a steady weekly cadence.
- [ ] Post value-first in two or three antique/estate-sale Facebook groups.

**Week 2+:**
- [ ] Add the direct PDF on Gumroad ($17 to $19) + a free lead-magnet cheat sheet for email capture.
- [ ] Once the listing shows reviews and organic sales, start the Amazon Ads test ($5 to $10/day,
      exact-match keywords + competitor titles). Scale what's profitable; kill the rest.

---

## What "good" looks like (honest)

- Father's Day window: a realistic 30 to 100 sales if you actively push your network and Pinterest;
  do not bank on a windfall from a cold start.
- The real win is the **evergreen tail**: steady monthly sales from Amazon search + Pinterest, plus a
  growing email list that you can sell the next thing to (the encyclopedia edition, the ID app).
